  • Join Us to Remember Thomas Sokolowski and Mark Day With(out) Art on December 1
    November 20, 2020
  • Join Us for Upcoming Virtual Programs
    November 19, 2020

    Visit go.rutgers.edu/December1 throughout the day for updates, including Day Without Tom memorial posts and the documentary short One Singular Sensation.

     

    The Zimmerli celebrates the life and achievements of its late director Thomas Sokolowski – an activist and arts leader since the early 1980s – during Art Before/After Hours, with live virtual programming, on December 1. Since 1989, the date has marked the annual observation of Day With(out) Art/World AIDS Day. This national day of action and mourning in response to the HIV/AIDS crisis was initiated by Visual AIDS, an organization that Sokolowski helped found.

     

    The morning session presents a live Zoom screening of TRANSMISSIONS, six videos commissioned by Visual AIDS that considers the global impact of HIV/AIDS. The evening session features a live Zoom panel discussion about the historical and contemporary intersections of HIV/AIDS advocacy and the arts. Throughout the day, look for additional content, including the debut of a short documentary about Sokolowski and memories submitted for Day Without Tom. Schedule and registration details are available at go.rutgers.edu/December1.

     

    This program is presented in collaboration with Rutgers Global, Mason Gross School of the Arts, Rutgers Student Health, Rutgers Global Health Institute, Center for Social Justice Education and LGBT Communities, and Office of the Chancellor, Rutgers – New Brunswick.
