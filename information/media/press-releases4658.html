  • Universitywide COVID-19 Information
    March 18, 2020

    Please follow the university's dedicated COVID-19 information page regarding closures, postponements, and all other updates across Rutgers campuses.

     

    In the interest of protecting the well-being of our staff, the university community, and all visitors, and following guidance from campus, state, and federal authorities regarding measures that best promote public health, we have decided to temporarily close the Zimmerli to the public until further notice. In addition, all previously scheduled programs and events at the university through the month of May are suspended.

     

    The most effective way to reach museum staff at this time is by email. Please visit the contact page and select the staff member's department. If you do not know his or her department, email info@zimmerli.rutgers.edu and include the name of the person you wish to reach, or a brief description of the information you seek, and your message will be directed to the appropriate individual. Thank you, for your understanding, as we all continue to adapt our daily routines when necessary.

     

    Like everyone at this time, we are actively monitoring COVID-19 as the circumstances continue to evolve. Any further updates will be announced on our website and social media channels.

     

    Please Note: PaparazZi Café is closed until further notice.

  • New Semester, New Experiences in Art
    January 31, 2020

    Everyday Soviet: Soviet Industrial Design and Nonconformist Art (1959-1989) opens Feburary 8, juxtaposing design objects, primarily from the Moscow Design Museum, with works by nonconformist artists from the Dodge Collection.

     

    Music at the Museum, presented by Rutgers Community Arts, pays tribute to the exhibition Intimate Details: Prints by James Tissot on February 9. The afternoon features the music that filled the cafés, salons, and concert halls of Paris during the second half of the 19th century, during the era of French society painter James Tissot.

     

    The above programs are free and open to the public. Please visit the link provided for each event to access important parking information.

     

    Now on View: The oil painting Two Peasant Women (1928-30) by Kazimir Malevich, a loan from the Moscow-based cultural project Encyclopedia of the Russian Avant-Garde, welcomes visitors into the George Riabov Gallery.

  • Autumn Programs for All Interests
    November 19, 2019

    Thinking about becoming a member? Learn more about such benefits as discounts at the Holiday Pop-Up Shop this weekend and on art classes - the next one is Winter Watercolors with Tom Rutledge on December 7.

     

    The new exhibition A Celebration of the Books of Vladimir Radunsky features illustrations from four of his children's books, including the newly released Mother Goose of Pudding Lane. Chris Raschka, who authored the book, stops by the museum on November 23 to read from it, share insights about writing, and discuss working with Vladimir Radunsky, his longtime friend. The program is free, but an RSVP is requested.

     

    Dialogues—Ilya Kabakov and Viktor Pivovarov: Stories About Ourselves, which delves into one of the hallmarks of unofficial Soviet art from the height of the Cold War, opens November 30. The exhibition focuses on the two artists’ work created in the format of the album: an innovative genre of visual art popularized in the 1970s by conceptual artists in Moscow. Save the date for a free, public reception on December 14.

     

    NOW OPEN: Home is Where… explores visual interpretations of domestic spaces.

     

    Holiday Hours: The Zimmerli is closed Thursday and Friday, November 28 and 29. The museum is open regular hours Saturday and Sunday, November 30 and December 1.
